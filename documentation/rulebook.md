CHRONICLES OF THE FALL: COMPLETE RULEBOOK

A Cooperative-Competitive Game of Imperial Decay for 3 to 5 Players

1. GAME CONCEPT & OBJECTIVE

1.1 The Premise

In Chronicles of the Fall, you and your fellow players represent the Ruling Council of a massive, fragile empire. Unlike traditional strategy games, there is no victory condition for the Empire itself. The systems are designed for inevitable systemic collapse. The Treasury will dry up, the people will revolt, or the borders will crumble. The game ends immediately when any of the three Global Pillars (Treasury, Stability, or Morale) drops to 0.

1.2 Winning the Game

While you must work together to prevent the Empire from collapsing too early, you are not a united front. Each player secretly represents an influential Faction with its own Hidden Agenda (e.g., the greedy Merchant Lords, the fanatic Cult of the End, or the defensive Old Nobility).

When the Empire finally collapses, players reveal their Hidden Agendas. If you have successfully steered the Empire’s development to match your Faction's specific criteria at the moment of collapse, you win. If multiple players qualify, the one who possesses the highest total value of cards remaining in their hand wins and writes the historical epilogue of the civilization. If no player meets their Agenda's conditions, history forgets you all.

2. THE ANATOMY OF SYSTEMIC MATH

To play effectively, you must understand the two distinct layers of the game's economy: Permanent Tags and Volatile Resources.

2.1 Permanent Tags (The Empire State)

Permanent Tags represent physical infrastructure, cultural ideologies, or geographic realities. They are printed on built cards and never exhaust.

Active Status: Even if a card is exhausted (tapped) during the Administration Phase to generate resources, its Permanent Tags remain fully active on the board.

Crisis Resolution: During Phase 3, the game evaluates the raw total of matching Permanent Tags on the board to calculate your baseline defense against incoming Events.

Example: A built Grand Garrison has two permanent [Military] tags. Even if the Garrison was exhausted earlier in the year to draw a card, it still contributes $2 \times \text{Military}$ to repel an incoming barbarian invasion.

2.2 Volatile Resources (Temporary Mana)

Volatile Resources represent immediate human effort, raw material flow, or liquid cash. They are generated dynamically during Phase 2 (the Administration Phase) by exhausting active cards.

The Volatile Pool: When you exhaust a building, the resources it generates go into your temporary personal pool.

Immediate Use or Loss: These resources cannot be saved or hoarded. They must be spent immediately on active Projects in the queue or allocated to cards accepting them. The moment you pass or propose a project to end your turn, your volatile pool is wiped to zero.

Example: You exhaust a Stone Quarry to generate 2 [Material]. You must immediately place those two tokens on an active Project in the construction queue. If you choose to Pass without spending them, those two [Material] tokens are returned to the supply.

3. CARD CATEGORIES

The Empire Deck and the Event Deck are composed of highly specific, interlocking card types.

3.1 Buildings (The City Infrastructure)

Buildings are physical components constructed within the City Graph.

Placement: Must be placed connected to a City Node.

Function: They provide permanent tags and can be exhausted once per Year during the Administration Phase to generate Volatile Resources or trigger unique card effects.

3.2 Politics & Economy (Instant Resolutions)

These cards represent laws, financial maneuvers, or swift military actions.

Restriction: These cards can only be proposed as Projects by the Minister of State.

Function: They do not stay on the board. When finalized, they instantly execute their effects (such as shifting the values of Global Pillars) and are immediately discarded.

Example: War Bonds immediately increases Treasury by 2, but reduces Morale and Stability by 1.

3.3 Event Cards (The Disasters)

Drawn from the Event Deck, these cards sit in the Event Queue, slowly crawling toward execution. Every Event card is structured as a comprehensive challenge:

Domain: Links the Event to a specific Ministry (e.g., Military -> Minister of War).

Defense Requirement: The exact combination and quantity of Defense Tags (e.g., [Military], [Sanitary], [Diplomacy]) required to successfully repel the disaster.

Sabotage Thresholds: Distinct penalty tiers triggered by [Unrest] cards played secretly by players during the Secret Pitch.

Minor Sabotage (e.g., 1 Unrest): Small infrastructure damage.

Major Sabotage (e.g., 2+ Unrest): Heavy penalties, automatic defense failure, or Minister demotions.

Success Reward (Optional): Grants resources, cards, or positive Pillar adjustments if successfully mitigated.

Failure Drawback: The severe structural penalty applied to the Empire's Pillars or active buildings if the Defense Requirement is not met.

4. GAME COMPONENTS & PLAY SPACES

4.1 Component List

1 Empire Deck: Consisting of Buildings, Politics, and Economy cards.

1 Event Deck: Consisting of Crisis and Final Epoch cards.

1 Common Project Pool: Physical cards representing basic, infinite blueprints (e.g., Basic Farm, Basic Quarry, Basic Garrison) accessible to all players.

5 Minister Cards: Role cards drafted each Year.

5 Player Aid Sheets / Secret Agenda Cards.

3 Tracking Tokens: Unique markers for Treasury, Stability, and Morale.

Volatile Resource Tokens: Multi-colored markers tracking [Labor], [Material], and [Wealth].

Pillar Board: A tracking board scale 0-10 for the three Global Pillars.

4.2 The Five Play Zones

The City Graph (The Map): The physical layout of the Empire. It starts with the Capital in the center. Cities are expanded by placing connections and building cards around them.

The Project Queue: A physical track with exactly 3 slots (Indices 0, 1, and 2). It functions as a strict First-In-First-Out (FIFO) queue for cards under construction.

The Empire Slots: A separate space on the board holding active, long-term political or economic policies that affect the entire Empire.

The Event Queue: A 3-step hidden track. Step 1 is the entry point (face-down), Step 2 is the middle, and Step 3 is the final step before the card drops into the Active Crisis Zone to be resolved.

Player Hands: The private, hidden cards held by each player. Players can communicate in generalities but may never show their cards to others.

5. SETUP (3-5 PLAYERS)

Initialize the Pillars: Place the Pillar Board in the center of the table. Set the Treasury, Stability, and Morale tracking markers to 5 (Equilibrium).

Found the Capital: Place the Capital City Node card in the center of the table. This acts as the physical center of the City Graph.

Establish the Common Projects: Place the Basic Farm, Basic Quarry, and Basic Garrison cards face-up to the side of the board. These are always available to be proposed as Projects.

Prepare the Decks:

Shuffle the Empire Deck and deal 3 cards face-down to each player as their starting hand.

Shuffle the Event Deck. Select one random Final Epoch card, place it face-down at the bottom of the deck, and place the rest of the Event Deck on top of it.

Build the Event Queue: Draw 3 Event cards from the top of the Event Deck and place them face-down into Step 1, Step 2, and Step 3 of the Event Queue. (Note: During Years 1 and 2, the Active Crisis Zone remains empty).

Distribute Agendas: Deal 1 Hidden Agenda card secretly to each player.

Select First Minister: The oldest player receives the Minister of the Empire role card to start the first Year.

6. THE YEAR STRUCTURE (DETAILED TURN PHASES)

Every round of the game represents one fiscal Year of the Empire.

PHASE 1: THE COUNCIL & THE SHADOWS

This phase establishes the year’s leadership, resources, and incoming threats.

The Event Scroll: The Event Queue advances.

The face-down card sitting in Step 3 is moved into the Active Crisis Zone (it will be flipped and resolved in Phase 3).

The card in Step 2 slides to Step 3.

The card in Step 1 slides to Step 2.

A new card is drawn face-down from the Event Deck and placed into Step 1.

The Imperial Block: The player holding the Minister of the Empire role selects one player. That player is blocked from the Council for the year. They cannot draft a Ministry and operate as a standard Citizen.

The Ministry Draft: Starting clockwise from the Minister of the Empire, all eligible players (excluding the blocked Citizen) select one available Ministry role card.

The Draw: All players (including the Citizen) draw exactly 1 card from the Empire Deck into their private hand.

Reconnaissance: The Minister of War may secretly peek at one face-down card in the Event Queue (either Step 1, 2, or 3) and return it face-down.

PHASE 2: THE ADMINISTRATION PHASE

Starting with the Minister of the Empire, players take turns in a clockwise direction. On your turn, you may execute Exactly One Primary Action and Any Number of Free Actions.

Primary Actions (Must choose exactly one):

Propose a Project: Select a card from your hand or the Common Pool and place it into Slot 2 (the entrance) of the Project Queue.

The FIFO Shift: If there is already a card in Slot 2, it slides to Slot 1, and Slot 1 slides to Slot 0. If a card is pushed past Slot 0, it is discarded, and all Volatile Resources assigned to it are permanently lost.

Special Restriction: Only the Minister of State can propose Political or Economic cards.

Ending Turn: Taking this action immediately ends your turn and flushes your temporary resource pool to zero.

Exhaust a Card: Choose one ready (untapped) Building on the board under your control. Turn it sideways to active its effect (e.g., generate Volatile Resources or draw cards).

Turn Continuity: This action does not end your turn. You may continue to take Free Actions (such as spending the resources you just generated).

Pass: Declare that you are done with your turn. This ends your turn, resets your volatile resource pool to zero, and increases the consecutive pass tracker.

Free Actions (May execute at any point during your turn):

Allocate Resources: Spend Volatile Resources currently in your temporary pool and place them onto a Project card in the Queue. You may only place matching resource types (e.g., you cannot place [Labor] on a slot requiring [Wealth]).

Finalize a Project: Only available to the Minister of the Empire and the Minister of Infrastructure. If a Project card in the Queue has accumulated resources matching its build cost, you may pay a Free Action to move it onto the board.

Buildings are placed on the map adhering to spatial connection rules.

Politics/Economy cards resolve their effects instantly and are discarded into the trash. If they enter an occupied Empire Slot, the old card is overwritten and discarded.

The Administration Phase ends immediately when all players pass consecutively.

PHASE 3: THE CRISIS RESOLUTION

If there is an Event card in the Active Crisis Zone, it must be resolved now. Flip the card face-up.

The Public Defense: Evaluate the built, active cards in the City Graph. Tally all matching permanent defense tags (e.g., [Military] for invasions, [Sanitary] for plagues). This is your starting defense pool. (Exhausted buildings still count).

The Public Edict: The Minister in charge of the Event's domain (e.g., Minister of War for a Military event) must place one of their double-sided Edict tokens on the Event. This alters the mechanical rules of the Crisis. If the relevant Minister is missing or was blocked this year, the Minister of the Empire chooses the Edict.

The Secret Pitch: Every player (including the blocked Citizen) must select exactly one card from their hand and place it face-down into the center of the table.

The Shuffle & Reveal: The face-down cards are thoroughly shuffled so they cannot be traced back to their owners. Flip the cards and evaluate only their bottom Commitment Strips.

The Sabotage Check: Count the number of [Unrest] tags revealed in the pitch. Check this number against the Event's Sabotage Threshold:

If the Unrest count meets a threshold, resolve the sabotage penalty immediately (e.g., destroying a building or losing resources).

Thresholds stack. If an Event has a 1 [Unrest] threshold and a 2+ [Unrest] threshold, revealing 2 [Unrest] resolves both thresholds in order. For example, if each threshold is -1 Morale, the Empire loses 2 Morale total.

The Final Resolution: Sum the Public Defense Tags + the positive Commitment Strip Tags + any Edict modifiers.

Success: If the total meets or exceeds the Event's Defense Requirement, the Event is defeated. Apply the Success Reward.

Failure: If the total is less than the Defense Requirement, the Event breaches the defenses. Apply the Failure Drawback (typically losing Global Pillar points).

Event Effect Types:

Generate Volatile Resources: Place the listed volatile resources into the personal pool of the Minister who has jurisdiction over the Event domain. These resources survive until the end of that player's next Administration turn, giving the Minister one turn to allocate them.

Modify Pillar: Increase or decrease Treasury, Stability, or Morale by the listed amount.

Destroy Building With Tag: Destroy one built Building with the listed permanent tag. The Minister of Infrastructure chooses which matching Building is destroyed. If there is no Minister of Infrastructure this year or that Minister is blocked, the Minister of the Empire chooses.

Discard Card: The target player discards the listed number of cards. The target can be all players or the player currently holding a specific Ministry.

Freeze Resource Generation: For the next Year, the listed volatile resource cannot be produced. Buildings may still be exhausted for their other effects, but any frozen resource they would generate is not added to a pool.

Block Minister Next Year: The player currently holding the listed Ministry is blocked from Council during the next Year. If that player would become Minister of the Empire, the sovereign role skips to the next eligible player and the blocked player remains a Citizen for that Year.

Clean Up: Discard all cards pitched into the Crisis Pool. Slide the completed Event to the Event Discard Pile.

7. THE MINISTRIES

7.1 Ministerial Powers and Jurisdictions

Ministries dictate who controls structural decisions during the Administration Phase and who holds executive power during the Crisis Phase.

Minister of the Empire (The Sovereign)

Jurisdiction: Oversees general state affairs. Decides Edicts for any Crisis whose Minister is absent or blocked.

Administration: Always takes the first turn of the Administration phase.

Free Action: May finalize fully funded Projects in the Queue.

Council: Selects one player to be blocked from holding office for the Year.

Minister of Infrastructure (The Builder)

Jurisdiction: Oversees geographic and structural expansion. (flavor text, no effect here)

Crisis Authority: Decides which matching Building is destroyed by any Event effect that destroys a Building with a specific tag. If the Minister of Infrastructure is missing or blocked, this decision passes to the Minister of the Empire.

Free Action: May finalize fully funded Projects in the Queue.

Free Action: Once per year, generate 1 [Labor], 1 [Material], or 1 [Wealth] into your volatile pool. (the list must be modifiable by admin)

Minister of War (The Defender)

Jurisdiction: Controls Defense Edicts for all [Military] events.

Council: Secretly looks at one face-down Event card anywhere in the Event Queue.

Minister of Health & Harvest (The Provider)

Jurisdiction: Controls Defense Edicts for all [Sanitary] and Nature events.

Minister of State (The Diplomat)

Jurisdiction: Controls Defense Edicts for all [Diplomacy]  events.

Administration: The only player permitted to propose Political or Economic cards into the Project Queue.

7.2 The Citizen (The Blocked Player)

Being blocked by the Minister of the Empire does not leave you powerless. 

Actions: You still take turns during the Administration Phase, draw a card during Phase 1, and must participate in the Secret Pitch.

8. PILLAR THRESHOLDS & CRISIS MODIFIERS

The values of the Global Pillars dynamically warp the rules of the game.

8.1 Treasury (0 to 10 Scale)

8–10 (Prosperity): Subsidized. The first Project finalized each Year costs 1 less resource of any type.

4–7 (Equilibrium): Standard rules.

1–3 (Crisis): Bankrupt. The Project Queue is physically reduced. Slot 0 is locked. Place the Treasury tracker over Slot 0 to block card placement. If a card was in Slot 0, it is immediately discarded.

8.2 Stability (0 to 10 Scale)

8–10 (Prosperity): Unified. During a Secret Pitch, automatically ignore the first [Unrest] card revealed.

4–7 (Equilibrium): Standard rules.

1–3 (Crisis): Fractured. Base corruption takes root. Every Secret Pitch automatically resolves with +1 [Unrest] pre-added to the pool.

8.3 Morale (0 to 10 Scale)

8–10 (Prosperity): Zealous. During Phase 1, all players draw 2 cards from the Empire Deck, choose 1 to keep, and discard the other.

4–7 (Equilibrium): Standard rules.

1–3 (Crisis): Despairing. Panic weakens coordination. The base Defense Requirement of all Events is increased by +1.

9. RULE CLARIFICATIONS & EDGE CASES

Permanent Tags vs. Exhaustion: Exhausting a building only prevents you from activating its manual action again in the same Year. It does not erase or exhaust the Permanent Tags printed on the card. Those tags are always active for defending against Events.

What is Food? [Food] is a specific tag checked by Sanitary and Nature Events. If an Event card reads "Looming Famine: requires 3 Sanitary. If the Empire has at least 2 active [Food] tags, reduce the requirement to 1."

Project Queue Discarding: When a new Project is proposed and pushes an existing unfinished project out of Slot 0, the pushed card is discarded to the bottom of the deck. All resource tokens on it are returned to the common supply.
